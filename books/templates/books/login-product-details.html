The George Akume Leadership Recruitment Model

The Nigerian state has failed from a formerly epistemic purity of the past and that there is the conflict of "truth" and "facts" as opposed to disencrypted fake news. Poor leadership recruitment process has aided corruption and instilled the culture of falsehood and fake news.

It has created a huge gap between the anticipated gains of democracy and democracy dividends on ground. This paper argues that the masses in Nigeria are like boats on the volatile sea without a leadership guide and that, democratic consolidation in Nigeria suffers too often because of the problem of failure of leadership.

Key Observations

  • Leadership recruitment process has been compromised
  • Democratic institutions are weakened by poor leadership selection
  • The gap between democratic promises and reality continues to widen

The ontology proposed in this paper seeks to address these fundamental challenges by reimagining the leadership recruitment model. It emphasizes the need for a more transparent, merit-based approach to selecting political leaders.

Proposed Solutions

  1. Implement rigorous candidate screening processes
  2. Establish clear leadership competency frameworks
  3. Create accountability mechanisms for political candidates

By addressing these critical areas, the Ayatutu Ontology aims to transform the current leadership paradigm and restore faith in democratic governance.

Definition of Ayatutu Ontology

Ayatutu Ontology refers to the conceptual framework and model developed in the paper, which examines the leadership recruitment process in Nigeria. It aims to analyze the factors that have led to the deterioration of democratic consolidation and the "anticipated gains of democracy" in the country. The ontology provides a critical appraisal of the leadership system and proposes a reformatting of the leadership recruitment model to allow for better democratic governance.
